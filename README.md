# SODH AI-powered Research Operating System Version: Product Brief v1.0

Overview SODH is an AI-powered Research Operating System designed to streamline the complete biomedical research workflow—from research ideation to publication. Instead of relying on multiple disconnected tools for literature management, collaboration, proposal development, screening, writing, and manuscript preparation, SODH aims to provide an integrated platform where researchers can efficiently plan, execute, and publish scientific research. The platform combines traditional research utilities with AI-assisted intelligence to reduce repetitive manual work while maintaining researcher oversight and scientific transparency.

Vision To build an intelligent research ecosystem that empowers students, clinicians, scientists, and academic institutions to conduct higher-quality research faster, with greater efficiency and collaboration. Rather than replacing researchers, SODH is designed to augment scientific thinking by automating routine workflows and accelerating evidence discovery.

Current Modules

    Literature Toolkit (Evidence Preparation) A standalone literature management module that simplifies evidence preparation before systematic reviews and research projects. Features • RIS/NBIB conversion • Multi-database merging • Intelligent deduplication • Duplicate review workspace • Screening-ready exports • Processing reports

    Research Suggestions (AI-powered Research Ideation) Generates structured research ideas from a user-provided biomedical topic within seconds using a lightweight AI workflow. Outputs include: • Research gaps • Emerging research topics • Future research directions • Potential SRMA topics • Cohort study ideas • Clinical study ideas • RCT concepts • AI/ML opportunities • Interdisciplinary research ideas Designed for rapid brainstorming rather than evidence synthesis.

    Synapse (Research Collaboration & Project Management) A collaborative research workspace enabling teams to organize, execute, and monitor research projects. Key capabilities include: • Research project creation • Team formation • Role assignment • Task allocation • Progress tracking • Accountability mechanisms • Integrated PubMed search with query expansion • Centralized project management Synapse is currently deployed as a standalone platform and is intended to become an integrated collaboration module within SODH.

Modules Under Development Deep Explore An evidence-backed research intelligence engine that moves beyond idea generation by analyzing published literature to identify: • Research gaps • Consensus and conflicting evidence • Underrepresented populations • Common methodological limitations • Future research opportunities • Potential SRMAs and clinical studies Every insight will be traceable to supporting scientific literature, ensuring transparency and reproducibility.

Planned Modules Proposal Builder AI-assisted generation of research proposals, protocols, ethics submissions, and grant applications.

Screening Assistant Support for title/abstract screening and full-text screening with transparent decision tracking and reviewer collaboration.

Escrib A scientific writing suite including: • Abstract writing • Manuscript drafting • Paraphrasing • Scientific language refinement

Reference Manager Automated citation formatting, bibliography generation, journal-specific reference styling, and reference validation.

Data Extractor (Long-term Vision) Structured extraction of study characteristics and outcome data from uploaded full-text articles to accelerate evidence synthesis.

Long-term Vision SODH aims to become a comprehensive Research Operating System that supports researchers throughout the complete research lifecycle: Research Idea ↓ Research Intelligence ↓ Team Formation & Collaboration ↓ Proposal Development ↓ Evidence Preparation ↓ Screening ↓ Data Extraction ↓ Scientific Writing ↓ Reference Management ↓ Publication

Guiding Principles • Researcher-in-the-loop AI • Evidence-backed outputs • Transparent and reproducible workflows • Modular and extensible architecture • Integration rather than fragmentation • Practical utility for students, clinicians, and researchers

Intended Users • Medical students • Postgraduate trainees • Clinician-scientists • PhD researchers • Academic institutions • Research organizations • Faculty mentors • Collaborative research groups

Mission To reduce the operational burden of biomedical research, allowing researchers to spend less time navigating fragmented workflows and more time generating meaningful scientific knowledge.
